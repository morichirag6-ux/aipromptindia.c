AI Sales Call Role-Play Simulator

Practice tough sales conversations before you talk to real buyers.

Build a role-play scenario in one click. Paste the prompt into ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude or Perplexity and let AI act as your buyer: founder, CTO, CFO, tenant, investor… even “very tough” modes.

  • 🎯 Choose industry, buyer role, and objective (book demo, close deal, renew, etc.)
  • 😈 Pick difficulty: easy, realistic, or aggressive
  • 🧠 Get a long, structured prompt + sample script

Free practice gym for SDRs, AEs, founders, and sales coaches.

Step 1 · Design your scenario

Takes ~20 seconds

Step 2 · Paste this into your AI

Tip: Ask the AI to “repeat the exercise but harder” or “give me feedback on my answers” after the first run.

Sample script (what the AI might do)

Scenario · B2B SaaS · Book a demo · Realistic difficulty

AI (CTO): Hey, I’ve got about 8 minutes before my next call. What do you do, and why should I care right now?

You (SDR): Totally get it, thanks for being direct. In one line: we help engineering teams like yours cut weekly status-report time by 50–60% without changing their current tools.

AI (CTO): Everyone says that. We already have Jira and some internal dashboards. Why would I add one more tool?

You (SDR): That’s fair. Instead of replacing Jira, we sit on top of it and auto-generate the updates your PMs are manually compiling today. Can I quickly ask how your team shares sprint progress with non-technical stakeholders?

AI (CTO): Right now, PMs spend Friday afternoons making slides. They complain, but it works… more or less.

After the call, ask the AI: “Give me feedback on my answers and how I could improve my discovery questions.”

How to use the simulator

  1. Pick your industry, buyer role, difficulty and objective.
  2. Add any extra notes (product, country, your experience).
  3. Click Generate role-play prompt.
  4. Paste the prompt into ChatGPT / Gemini / Claude.
  5. Type your answers as if it’s a real call. Ask for feedback at the end.

Pro tip: Record your answers and build a “best answers” library for your team.
